VR in the Media

"VR as a display technology... will be a replacement for all other forms of display technology, input and output. This is going to be our future."

~ Tim Sweeny

VR From Past to Present

Over the years, virtual reality has gone from being just a concept with similar properties as a panoramic

painting to what we know today. Virtual reality is utilized in many new media forms. As our society continues

to advance, virtual reality will replace most current usages of media.

VR as a Viewing Platform

Virtual reality is a unique way to view video games, television shows, and movies. This will further allow

important life moments to be captured as if you are there in real time. With VR, there is a unique 360° view.

VR Music

This technology has formed part of experimental sound displays and sound installations. Another use is virtual

reality musical instruments where the person can interact with these instruments as a new type of performance.

VR in film and TV

Virtual reality has been featured in several film and television programs. It is often used to illustrate the

concept of being trapped within the machine (i.e, cyberspace), or as a form of advanced technology.

VR Art

There are artists who use virtual reality to explore certain ideas or concepts. They create a three dimensional

environment as a form of communication with the audience. One example is the work of Kenneth Rinaldo

who uses robotics and augmented reality to explore ideas related to the human-technology boundary.

VR Case Studies

Jang Ji-sung, who lost her daughter to blood cancer in 2016, welcomed the opportunity to see her daughter

again—even if it was just in VR. Jang may not have been the first mother to lose a child, but she is perhaps

the first who got to meet her deceased daughter as a VR simulation, one that took almost a year to create.
