History of VR

Timeline

Virtual reality is utilized in many new media forms. As our society continues to advance, virtual reality

will replace most current usages of media.

Panoramic paintings

If we focus more strictly on the scope of virtual reality as a means of creating the illusion that we are present

somewhere we are not, then the earliest attempt at virtual reality are panoramic paintings.

1838 – Stereoscopic Photos & Viewers

The design principles of the Stereoscope is used today for the popular Google Cardboard and low budget VR head mounted

displays for mobile phones.

1929 – Link Trainer The First Flight Simulator

In 1929 Edward Link created the “Link trainer” (patented 1931) probably the first example of a commercial flight simulator,

which was entirely electromechanical.

1960s – The first VR Head Mounted Display

The Telesphere Mask was the first example of a head-mounted display, used for the non-interactive film medium without any

motion tracking. The headset provided stereoscopic 3D and wide vision with stereo sound.

1980s – Virtual reality the name was born & NASA joins the fun

In 1987 Jaron Lanier coined the term “virtual reality” and NASA creates Project VIEW. A VR simulator that was used to train astronauts.

2007 – Google Brings Us Street View

Google enhanced its Maps service with street-level 360-degree images, captured by special cars fitted with custom camera equipment.

Immersive Media did the work using a dodecahedral camera of their own design.

2020 – VR is Shifting Rapidly

Mixed Reality systems and sophisticated technologies are now part of standalone VR headsets. The Oculus Quest receives a promise of

tethering capability. The cost of VR headsets has dropped dramatically and more computers are now capable of running these headsets.
